## 🧪 Lab 1 — What Is a Prompt?

**A prompt is everything you type into an AI.** The quality of what you get back depends entirely on how well you ask.

> 🎯 **The Golden Rule of Prompting:**
> *Garbage in → Garbage out. Details in → Magic out.*

### The CRAFT Framework for better prompts:

| Letter | Stands For | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **C** | Context | "I'm a Year 9 student studying ancient history…" |
| **R** | Role | "Act as an expert museum curator…" |
| **A** | Action | "Explain the fall of the Roman Empire…" |
| **F** | Format | "…in 5 bullet points with an emoji per point" |
| **T** | Tone | "…using casual, friendly language" |

---

## 🎮 Lab 1 — Challenge 1: The "Bad Prompt → Good Prompt" Game

**Rules (work in pairs):**
1. Each pair gets a "bad prompt" card (below)
2. Rewrite it using the CRAFT framework
3. Test both the bad and good version — compare results!
4. Share the most dramatic improvement with the group

### Bad Prompt Cards:
```
Card A: "Tell me about space"
Card B: "Write a story"
Card C: "Help me with maths"
Card D: "What should I eat"
Card E: "Make me a quiz"
Card F: "Explain climate change"
```

### Example Transformation:
- ❌ **Bad:** `"Tell me about space"`
- ✅ **Good:** `"You are an astronomer talking to curious Year 8 students. Explain 3 mind-blowing facts about black holes using simple language, include an analogy for each, and finish with a question that makes them want to learn more."`

## 💬 Slide 7 — How Large Language Models Actually Work

> Imagine you're completing this sentence: *"The sky is ___"*

Your brain immediately jumps to "blue" or "cloudy" — because you've seen those words together millions of times.

**That's exactly what a Large Language Model (LLM) does** — but it's seen *trillions* of examples, not millions.

### The Transformer Revolution (2017)

```
Input Text → Tokenise → Self-Attention → Predict Next Token → Output
   "I love"  →  [I][love]  →  "How do these        → "pizza"
                               words relate?"
```

**Key Ideas:**
- 📖 **Tokens**: AI doesn't read letters — it reads "chunks" of text (~¾ of a word on average)
- 🔗 **Attention**: AI looks at ALL previous words to predict the next one
- 🎲 **Temperature**: Controls how creative/random the AI is (0 = predictable, 1 = wild)

> 🤔 **Think about it:** GPT-4 has ~1.76 trillion parameters (knobs it adjusts when learning). Your brain has ~100 trillion synapses. We're getting closer!

## 🖼️ Slide 8 — How AI Generates Images

> From random noise to a masterpiece in seconds — here's the magic:

### Diffusion Models (the tech behind DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, Midjourney)

```
Random Noise 🌫️  →  [AI adds structure step by step]  →  Final Image 🖼️
     ████         →          ░▒▓█▓▒░                →    🐶 in space
  (pure noise)       (gradual denoising)               (your prompt!)
```

**How it works (simple version):**
1. The AI learns by *adding noise* to millions of images until they look like static
2. Then it learns to *reverse* that process — "dreaming" an image from static
3. Your text prompt guides which direction the dream goes

### Key terms:
- **Prompt**: What you want in the image
- **Negative prompt**: What you DON'T want ("no blur, no text, no watermark")
- **Style modifiers**: "oil painting", "photorealistic", "anime style", "8-bit pixel art"
- **Seed**: A number that makes your generation reproducible

## 🎙️ Slide 9 — How AI Hears and Speaks

> Your voice is just a pattern of sound waves. AI turns those waves into text, meaning, and action.

### Speech-to-Text (Transcription)
```
Your Voice 🎤  →  Spectrogram (visual sound pattern)  →  Tokens  →  Text
  "Hello"            ▁▂▅▇▅▂▁                           [Hel][lo]  →  "Hello"
```

### Text-to-Speech (Voice Synthesis)
```
Text 📝  →  Phonemes  →  Neural voice model  →  Audio waveform  →  🔊
"Hello"  →  [HE][LOH] →  (trained on hours        → natural-sounding
                          of human speech)           voice output
```

### Voice Cloning (the exciting & scary bit):
- **2020:** Needed hours of your voice samples to clone it
- **2023:** Needed ~3 minutes
- **2024:** Needed ~15 seconds
- **2026:** Some models can clone from a single sentence

> ⚠️ **Real talk:** This is why "deepfake audio" is dangerous — and why digital verification matters more than ever.

**Round 4 — Code-switching:**

- Does the AI handle both languages?

**Discuss:** Where does speech AI still struggle? What would make it better?
